Problem Statement Core Challenge

Optimizing Harvest vs. Degrading FCR

Commercial broiler operations in Malaysia struggle to pinpoint the exact day of optimal harvest. Harvesting too early results in under-developed breast muscle, while keeping birds too long wastes expensive feed (imported corn/soy), degrading the Feed Conversion Ratio (FCR) and increasing mortality under extreme tropical humidity.

🎯 Objective: Use the growth curve derivative $f'(x)$ to find the exact day where growth velocity reaches its absolute maximum (the inflection point), identifying the optimal window to transition feeding regimes.
Bioscience Context SFH Core Elective

Non-Linear Growth Kinetics

In commercial poultry husbandry (e.g., Ross 308 / Cobb 500 performance guidelines in Malaysia), the biological growth curve is non-linear. Initially, day-old chicks grow slowly (brooding phase). They enter an exponential phase, and eventually reach a metabolic plateau where muscle deposition slows down and Feed Conversion Ratio (FCR) degrades.

💡

Why Derivatives Matter: The derivative $f'(x)$ represents the Average Daily Gain (ADG) in g/day. Calculating the peak of this derivative determines the inflection point of the growth curve.
